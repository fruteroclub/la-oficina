Today we made the repository for **La Oficina** public — the operating system we're using to build **Frutero Agency**.

Frutero is no longer just a community. It's now two things at once: a **Talent Accelerator** that trains operators specialized in AI, and an **agency** that sells their work to clients. The same people we accelerate inside are the ones who deliver outside. The accelerator trains. The agency earns. Surplus flows back to the accelerator.

Why it matters: traditional agencies never scaled well because senior talent is scarce, expensive, and uncloneable. AI changes the math: one human Architect + N AI Operators can deliver what used to require a team of N+1 humans. The Architect is still scarce, but stops being the delivery bottleneck.

For that to work you need a clear operating system. La Oficina is exactly that: an explicit hierarchy of agency → pod → workspace → project → workstream, with context any agent can load with one command, journaling that survives between sessions, and a clear contract between human and agent about who decides what.

And because building in private doesn't earn trust, all of this happens in public. Code, decisions, mistakes, daily journals. Our first Operator is named **Dumbleclaw**. It runs on the Hermes harness. It's learning to operate real pods with real clients.

If you're curious how an AI-native agency operates from the inside, or how a builder community can monetize without losing what makes it a community — worth following along.
